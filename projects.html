Projects

Applied work across airports, regions, and institutions.

My project portfolio combines aviation systems analysis, GIS, simulation, statistical modeling, and policy-oriented synthesis.

01

AAM integration decision support

A GIS-based framework that separates safety feasibility from sustainability evaluation for Advanced Air Mobility integration at U.S. large hub airports.

Doctoral research
  • Safety constraint screening
  • EONS trade-off evaluation
  • Scenario ranking and sensitivity analysis
02

Metropolitan AAM evidence base

A critical synthesis of research on airport-relevant AAM deployment, with emphasis on analytical fragmentation, metropolitan readiness, and air-ground system coupling.

Systematic review
  • PRISMA review methods
  • Multi-label coding
  • Bibliometric and thematic synthesis
03

Institutional capacity and aviation safety

A cross-national panel study examining how institutional capacity, conflict, fragility, and economic conditions relate to aviation accident occurrence.

Global safety
  • 171-country analytical panel
  • Count and zero-inflated models
  • Bayesian and spatial robustness analysis
04

eVTOL operational concepts

Discrete-event simulation of manual, semi-automated, and fully automated operating concepts for emerging eVTOL and vertiport systems.

Simulation
  • Arena simulation
  • Throughput and delay comparison
  • Operational scenario analysis
05

FODXplore

A human-centered design concept for autonomous foreign object debris detection in airport environments.

Aviation technology
  • Double Diamond design process
  • Airport safety application
  • Autonomous inspection concept
06

GIS and cartographic work

Spatial analysis and visual communication projects involving transportation, public health, environmental conditions, land cover, and airport systems.

Geospatial analysis
  • Time-enabled mapping
  • Spatial suitability analysis
  • Publication-quality cartography

Good transportation research crosses disciplinary boundaries.
