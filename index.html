About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Aviation and Aerospace Technology in the Computational Science and Engineering track at Purdue University. My doctoral studies are supported by the Frederick N. Andrews Fellowship.

My work focuses on aviation safety, transportation systems, transport policy, and the integration of emerging aviation technologies. I use computational, geospatial, and systems methods to address practical aviation and transportation problems.

I hold a B.E. in Aerospace Engineering and an M.S. in Aviation Engineering, and I have received several academic excellence awards. Before beginning my doctoral studies, I worked in aviation safety, quality, security, and regulatory oversight in South Sudan.

Research

My research examines aviation as both a technical system and public infrastructure. My current work focuses on three related areas:

01

Advanced Air Mobility and airport integration

Assessing where, when, and under what safety and sustainability conditions AAM operations could be integrated at large U.S. hub airports.

02

Aviation safety and institutional capacity

Examining how regulation, conflict, state capacity, infrastructure, and system resilience shape aviation-safety outcomes across countries.

03

Spatial evidence for transport policy

Using GIS, scenario analysis, and multi-criteria methods to make safety constraints, accessibility, and public trade-offs visible to decision-makers.

Methods: ArcGIS Pro, spatial screening, AHP and TOPSIS, sensitivity analysis, panel count models, Bayesian analysis, simulation, systematic review, and thematic synthesis.
