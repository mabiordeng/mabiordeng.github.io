About my work

“The question is not only whether a new aviation technology can fly, but whether it can be integrated safely and serve the wider transport system.”

I am a PhD student in Aviation and Aerospace Technology in the Computational Science and Engineering track at Purdue University. My work brings together aviation engineering, airport systems, geographic information systems, quantitative decision analysis, and transport policy.

I am especially interested in practical integration problems: where emerging operations could fit, what safety conditions must be satisfied, how benefits and burdens are distributed, and whether technical efficiency translates into public value.

Before doctoral study, I worked in aviation safety, quality, security, and regulatory oversight in South Sudan. That experience continues to shape my interest in institutional capacity, safety disparities, and transport development in resource-constrained settings.

What I work on

Three connected lines of inquiry.

01

Emerging aviation and airports

How can Advanced Air Mobility and other emerging operations be integrated into busy airport and metropolitan systems without overlooking airspace, infrastructure, access, and governance?

02

Safety and institutional capacity

How do regulation, state capacity, conflict, infrastructure, and system resilience shape aviation safety outcomes across countries and over time?

03

Spatial evidence for policy

How can GIS, scenario analysis, and multi-criteria methods make safety constraints and public trade-offs more transparent to planners and decision-makers?
